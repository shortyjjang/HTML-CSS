Arturo Elías Ayub: Family Man

How one of the world's most powerful business men keeps his family first

Shop Arturo's Picks

This business story began on Calle del Correo Mayor, in a popular area of ​​an old commercial district, in the heart of the Historic Center of Mexico City. Although not a glamorous place, for Arturo Elías Ayub it represents where he learned the best lessons from the man who inspired him most: his father, Alfredo Elías.

At the age of seven, the youngest son of three siblings came in the afternoon to the industrial uniforms factory that his father was running. When Don Alfredo was distracted, Arturo did not miss the opportunity to escape and go to buy pencils, notebooks and rubber at wholesale and then sell them among his school friends.

"I went to his business and I ran away because the mega stationery was next door and I bought everything. I learned at Correo Mayor, the best university in Mexico," jokes Arturo Elías Ayub, director of Strategic Alliances and Content of América Móvil.

In addition to the factory, the head of the patriarchy had TV stores in the same central area of ​​the capital. Although Arturo attended classes, he asserts that the best lessons were learned alongside Don Alfredo, not only as his father, but also as his boss, with whom he worked shoulder to shoulder for several years.

Though Arturo certainly considers himself a "lucky man," for not having gone through any serious crisis in his five decades of life, there is a subject that always changes his face: the death of his father. Arturo was 22 years old when Don Alfredo died. 

The Mexican businessman does not forget that day, and although he is reserved to talk about it, that moment of impact helped him to mature and to define the strength of his character and clarity when making decisions. Arturo Elías Ayub defines himself as a person of family and values. For Arturo, honesty is something that is taken seriously in a personal and business environment, and he even considers this value as the compass that governs his life.

The director of Uno TV shares that the best teaching he received from his father was to always put the family above all else, including business. "I'm trying to put my priorities in a very clear balance. For me, my number one is God and my family. I have no doubts, I have it very clear and I've always had it clear in my mind."

Arturo works every day to convey to his three children the intangible heritage that Don Alfredo instilled in him since his days of buying and selling school supplies. Part of that is communicated through sports, one of the greatest passions of Elías Ayub.

Honesty, family and love of Mexico are the three pillars that govern the life of one of the men closest to Carlos Slim. And although transmitting these qualities to three children is not a simple task, he says he has the support of the best life companion to comply with that amendment.

Arturo Elías Ayub assures that from an early age he knew he was destined to be part of the business world. His first incursions were as an entrepreneur in his childhood, thanks to the impulse given by his father, that man whom he remembers and misses with great affection and admiration.

"It's something you bring in. You know whether or not you're an entrepreneur, as you know if you're an opera singer or a footballer, you just know if you are or you are not."
