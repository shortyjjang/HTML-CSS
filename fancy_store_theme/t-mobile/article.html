Legere's List T-Mobile's CEO John Legere shares his fall must-haves!

iPhone 7 Cases For Your T-Mobile Phone
By Nick Drake Photographer Roberto Grande

John Legere isn’t like other CEOs. In fact, he isn’t like anyone else, period. No matter how you feel about him, it’s impossible to ignore him. But this is one of the key reasons he is uniquely positioned to lead T-Mobile against larger competitors like Verizon and AT&T.

Under Legere’s leadership, T-Mobile has watched itself grow customers, revenue, and maybe more importantly, mind-share. It has been his “uncarrier” strategy that has blitzed competitors and won the hearts of new customers, making T-Mobile the fastest growing wireless carrier in the country.

"Declare victory, designate an enemy. Attack that enemy. The bigger the enemy, the better."

From getting rid off international roaming fees to offering to pay off customers’ AT&T contracts if they join T-Mobile, Legere has aggressively pursued his vision of a new kind of company.

And the enemy is paying attention. There was much speculation that Verizon’s recent acquisition of AOL was a response to the increasing pressure Legere and T-Mobile are putting on them. T-Mobile isn’t the only thing Legere has been reinventing. Since stepping into the CEO role at T-Mobile, Legere has been reinventing himself.

"Declare victory, designate an enemy. Attack that enemy. The bigger the enemy, the better."

A “corporate suite” in a former life, he has found his groove as a voice of the customer and an industry critic. This is a position that was clearly on display at the Consumer Electronics Show in Las Vegas in 2013, an event which many now see as Legree's stake in the ground.

It was this off-the-cuff, brutally honest keynote that had a CNET reporter Tweeting "T-Mobile CEO John Legere is killing it," and made PC magazine say, "Holy moley John Legere is the UnCarrierCEO.”

Despite being the nations fastest growing wireless carrier, there is still work to be done to out manuever his competitors. Legere has T-Mobile moving at lightening speed, and he’s showing no signs of slowing down anytime soon. Safe to say, the "uncarrier" movement is just getting started.
