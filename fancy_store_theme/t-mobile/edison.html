Edison bulbs, which refer to carbon or early tungsten-filament lamps. can easily be identified by the intricate windings of their internal filaments and the warm-yellow glow they produce. The original design by Edison Electric Light Company, first introduced to the consumer market in 1882, featured carbon filaments. In 1904, the bulb was re-outfitted with tungsten filaments.
